Class, Labor, and Political Economy

1938 – Fair Labor Standards Act

The Fair Labor Standards Act was a labor law signed by President Franklin D. Roosevelt in 1938. The Act transformed the American workforce by establishing the first federal minimum wage, a maximum weekly work hours, and a prohibition on child labor. This Act is known as one of the last measures of FDR’s New Deal and is directly connected to the Wagner Act of 1935, which protected the rights of workers to organize and bargain collectively. The Fair Labor Standards and the Wagner Act both shifted the federal government’s role in responding to workers’ needs from laissez-faire policies to protecting workers’ rights. The Fair Labor Standards Act demonstrates the government's pivot toward federal intervention to ensure economic stability and workers' rights for everyday Americans.
