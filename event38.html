1929 – Stock Market Crash

The United States Stock Market crashed on October 29, 1929, known as “Black Tuesday,” and erased billions of dollars in wealth. The crash highlights the concept of a “speculative bubble” fueled by buying stocks on margin, a practice where investors purchase shares with borrowed money from banks to chase unsustainable profits. When the stock market collapsed, investors were unable to pay back these loans, which in turn triggered massive bank failures and shattered consumer demand. The Stock Market Crash demonstrated the vulnerability of the unchecked credit-driven financial system in the 1920s and ultimately kick-started the Great Depression.
