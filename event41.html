2022 – Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization

Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization was a Supreme Court ruling decided on June 24, 2022, that ruled that the Constitution does not confer a right to abortion. This law overturned the Roe v. Wade (1973) and Planned Parenthood v. Casey (1992) laws and returned authority to regulate abortions back to states. The 14th Amendment was the primary constitutional basis for Roe v. Wade due to its Due Process Clause, which provides a fundamental “right to privacy,” but later, in Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization, it was argued that the Clause protects only “procedural” rights, not “substantive” rights. Because of this, the ruling raises concerns about whether other privacy-based rights might now be vulnerable to reversal. The Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization ruling shows how the government can perpetuate inequality and directly affect the working class, specifically working-class women who already face systemic barriers in accessing quality healthcare, forcing them to choose between carrying unwanted pregnancies to term and jeopardizing their economic stability.
