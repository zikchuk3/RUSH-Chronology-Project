Class, Labor, and Political Economy

1911 – Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire

The Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire occurred on March 25, 1911, in New York City, resulting in the death of more than 100 garment workers. The factory owners had routinely locked exit doors to prevent theft and breaks from work, trapping a lot of workers in the building. The tragic event caused outrage across the nation, inspiring people like Frances Perkins, who witnessed the fire, to spend their lifetimes advocating for workers’ rights. The Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire brought to light the horrible working conditions that workers faced and caused New York to establish the New York Factory Investigating Commission, work safety laws, and factory regulations that laid the groundwork for modern-day labor laws.
