Class, Labor, and Political Economy

1889 – Hull House

Hull House was the first English social settlement home in the United States, founded in Chicago, Illinois, in 1889 by Jane Addams and Ellen Gates Starr. The Hull House’s main objective was to provide community service, education, and social services to disadvantaged populations. It also exposed concerning conditions in local sweatshops and advocated for the organization of workers. Hull House is an early example of how communities of working-class citizens came together to give each other aid and fight for change in the system that upheld the social and economic problems they faced.
