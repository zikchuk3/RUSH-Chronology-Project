Class, Labor, and Political Economy

1894 – Pullman Strike

The Pullman Strike was a widespread railroad boycott and strike in 1894, initiated by workers at the Pullman Palace Car Company in Illinois in response to unfair wage cuts. Led by Eugene V. Debs and the American Railway Union (ARU), the protest halted rail traffic across 27 states, ultimately resulting in federal intervention by President Grover Cleveland to break the strike. The Pullman Strike was one of the many union strikes during the Gilded Age, when laborers advocated for better wages, shorter hours, and better working conditions. The Pullman Strike showed the immense power imbalance between workers and corporate interests in the Gilded Age.
