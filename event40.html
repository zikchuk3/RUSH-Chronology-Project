Class, Labor, and Political Economy

1969 – Stonewall

The Stonewall Rebellions began on June 28, 1969, when police raided the Stonewall Inn, a popular gay bar in Greenwich Village, New York. This led to pretests by members of the LGBTQ+ community and kickstarted the Gay Liberation Movement. The Stonewall Rebellions connect directly to the Compton's Cafeteria Riot of 1966, an earlier uprising in San Francisco driven by transgender women and drag queens resisting systemic police brutality. Both events illustrate how marginalized communities moved from passive endurance to acts of resistance against the discrimination they faced. The Stonewall Rebellions represent a major turning point when LGBTQ+ individuals publicly unified to demand civil rights, shifting the trajectory of the fight for LGBTQ+ rights from local to nationwide.
