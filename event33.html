1848 – Seneca Falls Convention

The Seneca Falls Convention was the first women's rights convention in the United States, held on July 19-20, 1848, in Seneca Falls, New York. This event was organized by Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Lucretia Mott and resulted in 300 attendees. At the convention, the “Declaration of Sentiments” was introduced by Stanton, modeled after the Declaration of Independence, and argued that the same rights that were deemed inalienable in 1776 should apply to women as well. The Seneca Falls Convention was the first time women collectively came together to advocate for change and launched the women’s suffrage movement, which demanded social, civil, and political equality for women.
